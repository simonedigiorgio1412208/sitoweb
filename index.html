About

I am a Research Scientist at the Istituto di Ingegneria del Mare (CNR-INM) in Rome, where I work on Direct Numerical Simulation of turbulent multiphase flows. My research sits at the intersection of fundamental fluid dynamics and engineering applications.

I completed my PhD in 2021 at Sapienza University of Rome under the supervision of Sergio Pirozzoli and Alessandro Iafrati. I have since been PI of multiple large-scale HPC allocations (>22M CPU-hours) on EuroHPC infrastructure.

I develop two in-house CFD codes: SALACIA, a structured Cartesian solver in Fortran/MPI, and URSULA, an unstructured GPU-native solver, both designed for petascale multiphase flow simulation.
